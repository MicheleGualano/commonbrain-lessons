An unquoted heredoc (<<EOF) expands $(...) and backticks in its body
#shell #bash #heredoc #gotcha #silent-failure
Rule. An unquoted heredoc (<<EOF) expands $var, $(...) and backticks in its body, so prose containing them silently runs as commands; quote the delimiter (<<'EOF') to make the body fully literal, but that also disables ${var}. When you need both literal metacharacters and real interpolation, assemble the string with printf or jq --arg instead of relying on <<EOF.

Triggers

  • bash: command not found from a word inside a heredoc body
  • cat <<EOF executed a backtick or $(...) instead of keeping it literal
  • warning: here-document delimited by end-of-file
  • text built with <<EOF got mangled or ran a command unexpectedly
