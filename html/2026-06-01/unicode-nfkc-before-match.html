Normalize and strip unicode before keyword matching or filters are trivially evaded
#unicode #security #normalization #homoglyph #content-filtering
Rule. Naive keyword/regex filters are bypassed by fullwidth forms, look-alike homoglyphs from other scripts (Cyrillic, Greek, Cherokee, Armenian), zero-width separators, and combining marks that split a banned word; NFKC- or NFKD-normalize the input, drop format (Cf) and combining (Mn/Mc/Me) characters, then match, and flag any token that mixes alphabets from more than one script.

Triggers

  • keyword filter missed a banned word written in fullwidth characters
  • homoglyph from Cyrillic/Greek slipped past the regex filter
  • zero-width space split the matched token so the filter saw nothing
  • combining mark diacritic evaded the banned-word check
  • mixed-script token was not flagged before matching
  • match worked only after unicodedata.normalize NFKC
