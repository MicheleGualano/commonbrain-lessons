After mirroring a repo (origin=your fork, upstream=theirs), do not git pull blind
#git #remotes #fork #mirror #upstream #merge
Rule. Once you mirror a third-party repo and remap remotes so origin is your mirror and upstream is theirs, your local main diverges hard from upstream; a blind `git pull` merges upstream's history onto your commits and creates a tangle. Instead `git fetch upstream` deliberately, decide how to integrate, and `git push origin` to back up your mirror.

Triggers

  • git pull created an unexpected merge from upstream onto my commits
  • Your branch and 'upstream/main' have diverged
  • have diverged, and have N and M different commits each, respectively
  • git pull on a mirrored repo pulled in the upstream's full history
  • origin points to my mirror but pull tracked upstream
  • Merge made by the 'ort' strategy after an unintended upstream pull
